What is Gamification?
Gamification is defined as “the use of game-mechanics in a non-game setting”…YAWN. How can anything with the word “game” in it sound so boring? Here’s a better definition:
Gamification is a method of influencing behavior by increasing motivation.
Think about professional athletes, yes, they get paid lots of money, but they also love to play the game. They are motivated by more than money. They want to be great performers, they want to contribute to the team, they want to get better and better. Like sports, gamification combines extrinsic rewards like prizes and money, with intrinsic rewards like pride, achievement, and contribution, among others.
How does it work?
Gamification can take many forms including:
  • Sales Competitions
  • Points
  • Personal Records
  • Prize Wheels
  • Milestones
  • Goals
  • Streaks
  • Challenges
